Town Layout: A small medieval crossroads village

The town is built around a dirt crossroads. Roads lead north, south, east, and west.

NORTH — Church & Graveyard
  The wooden church stands at the top of the village with Father Cuthbert stationed outside.
  To the right of the church to the northeast sits the graveyard, fenced in with a simple wooden fence.
  The ghosts Jeff and Mildred haunt the graveyard. Or, just chill there.

WEST — Houses
  A cluster of small houses lines the western road. Generic townsfolk live here.

EAST — Houses & Inn
  More houses along the eastern road. The tavern sits at the northeast corner of the
  crossroads, where Bertram the innkeeper works.

CENTER — The Well
  The well marks the center of the crossroads. Agnes the old lady stands near it,
  waiting for someone to talk to her.

SOUTHWEST — The Field
  A large crop field enclosed by fences. Cornelius the scarecrow guards it from birds.

SOUTHEAST — The Farm
  A fenced-off farm area with the barn, the farmhouse, and the three pigs:
  Hamlet, Truffles, and Sir Oinksworth the Third.

SOUTH — Entrance
  The pigeon enters the town from the very south, walking up toward the crossroads.

Landmarks characters might reference:
  - the church, the graveyard, the well, the tavern/inn, the field, the farm
  - the crossroads as the center of town
  - muddy paths connecting everything
  - forest in the north west
